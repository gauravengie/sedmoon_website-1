Building

The pilots : #3a, #3b, and #3c concern two distinct but related domains: Building and HVAC.  This module, concerns only the Building side.       

The pilot #3a: aims to meet two goals in an office building. On the one hand, it aims to optimize the control of the HVAC regarding occupancy. On the other hand, it aims to provide a demand response service through HVAC control. For this Pilot, it is necessary to be able to represent the building and its zones. In addition to the zone properties such as occupancy, indoor temperature, and humidity. The HVAC elements are represented in the HVAC module.   

Pilot #3b: aims to acquire, aggregate, and process energy consumption and related data of different buildings (often in various forms not coherent between them) to make energy domain-specific data analysis as consumption forecasting, predictive maintenance, benchmarking, and so on. Four different destinations for the building spaces are considered: Datacentre, Logistics, Retail, and Offices (Directional), for a total of 16 buildings organized in groups.  

Indoor and outdoor zones are also distinguished for lighting consumption and according to climate zone. 

Pilot #3c:  takes place in Nanogune, a tertiary sector smart building dedicated to nanotechnology research, based in San Sebastian (Spain). This building is divided into different areas such as offices and laboratories and has both thermal and electrical meters to differentiate the areas. 

In addition of representing the different zones in the building, properties such as comfort and occupancy are also required to be represented. 

Then the building ontology in Platoon project, aims to meet the need identified in these previous pilots  by  analyzing a detailed description given by each use case according to IEC-62559 template.

Mainly, seas building ontology is used and extended to complete the missing concepts meeting the pilots needs.
