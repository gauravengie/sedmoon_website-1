The Platoon Project

Platoon Project is an H2020 EU Funded project, it aims to digitalize the energy sector, enabling thus higher levels of operational excellence with the adoption of disrupting technologies.   

To reach this objective, one of the main issues among others, is to resolve the semantic interoperability between heterogeneous systems. For this purpose, a common semantic data model was designed to meet the need for interoperability.   

Ensuring the semantic interoperability issues between heterogeneous systems is a very challenging task. Considering the use of a common semantic data model is a central element to ensure this semantic interoperability. In fact, a common semantic data model allows heterogeneous systems to share the same meaning of entities that enables them to interoperate when they come to use them in their different processes and services 

We can notice that the Common semantic Data Model is closely related to the needs expressed in 19 use cases of the 7 pilots in different domains (Building, HVAC, Wind Turbine, Smart Grid, etc… ). Consequently, the different domains conceptualized, in the project, may not cover the whole scope of these domains.   

To design the CSDM the reusing of existing ontologies was privileged to respect the good practice in ontologies design principles. One concept is added when it does not already exist in identified ontologies (SAREF, CIM, SEAS, BOT, etc…) or when the meaning and the properties of the concept do not meet the need of the use case.
