Wind Turbine

Wind turbine ontology describes wind turbine and their components such as the generator and power converter and subcomponents such as rotor and stator. There are two different types of wind turbines: onshore wind turbine (plt:OnshoreWindTurbine) which is located on land, and offshore wind turbine (plt:OffshoreWindTurbine) which is located in bodies of water. Three key components of wind turbine are defined in this module; blade (plt:Blade), nacelle (plt:Nacelle), convertor (plt:Convertor). This module defines also Wind farm (plt:WindFarm) that is composed of a group of wind turbine in the same location used to produce electricity. The wind turbine is related to the wind farm by the (seas:isMemberOf) relation.

Use Cases: 

  • Predictive maintenance of wind farms 

  • Service for forecasting the power output of wind farms, the related uncertainties, and the optimal use of wind power in power system operations in order to facilitate large-scale integration of wind generation.
