Overview

Defining the scope of an ontology is one of the first questions to respond to, before starting the definition of the ontology. The Scope of the Platoon common semantic data model is use cases oriented. This means that the goal was not to build ontologies to cover the whole domains related to the Energy sector, but it was to meet the specific needs of scenarios defined by stakeholders in the Platoon projects.  

As a part of the Platoon project, 7 pilots with 19 use cases were defined as project validation.      

A brief description of each pilot is given below. After analyzing the different pilots, 4 distinct topics are identified to cover the scope of pilots and a central topic that is shared by the different pilots (see fig below). 

 

In order to reach the objective to create the Common Semantic Data model in the Platoon project. A hybrid bottom-up and top-down methodology is followed respecting  the ontological design principles and reusing design patterns.  

Reusing existing ontologies is one of the principles respected in the methodology.  

For this reason, in this work, one might find an extensive reusing of external concepts or at least mapping to external concepts in the case where others specific needs in uses cases, should be represented.
